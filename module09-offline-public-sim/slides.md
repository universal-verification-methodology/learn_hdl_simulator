# Free practice in the public IDE

No graded browser lab this time

---

## A simple practice loop
- Paste or open a tiny counter plus testbench
- Compile or elaborate until Console is clean
- Run a short window, Stop, Reset once
- Step a few times
- Add clock and q to the wave, place a cursor, and optionally poke a data net then release
- Stop when the loop feels boring, that is fluency

---

## What success looks like
- Success is not a score
- Success is: you can open the IDE without hunting for panes, you can control time
- If you get stuck, revisit the matching hdl-sim literacy lab, then return to the public IDE

---

## Optional depth
- Stretch: mis-set top on purpose, read the Console error, fix it
- Or force a net, watch the hazard, release it
- Or compare q at cursor one to a handwritten golden
- Keep the design tiny so the IDE skills stay the focus

---

## Pitfalls to watch
- Do not turn free practice into a day-long RTL rewrite
- Do not skip Console when something fails
- Do not leave forces active
- And do not confuse this browser IDE with offline Icarus or Verilator fidelity
